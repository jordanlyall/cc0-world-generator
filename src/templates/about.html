About Worldkit

What this is

Worldkit is a CC0 world generator. Give it a genre or theme — "noir detective city," "deep ocean mythology," "post-collapse salvage punk" — and it produces a structured World Bible: characters, factions, locations, tone, and a compliance manifest that traces every creative element back to a verified public-domain or CC0 source.

The output is designed to be safe to build on commercially, by humans and AI agents alike.

The corpus

Every world is assembled from a locked set of verified CC0 universes. Currently seven are included: Nouns, CrypToadz, mfers, racc00ns, XCOPY, Loot (for Adventurers), and Bulfinch's Mythology (1855 edition, globally public domain). Each universe has a corresponding evidence file recording the declaration source, date, and scope. Nothing enters the corpus without a traceable license event.

How declaration quality is evaluated

Not all CC0 declarations are equal. Each universe in the corpus carries a confidence level computed from its evidence chain — not set manually. The logic is simple:

Level What it means
high Explicit CC0 declaration from the copyright holder (or clear public domain), primary evidence captured, no trademark flags above low, no jurisdiction ambiguity.
medium CC0 confirmed but a trademark or meme-derivative flag is present, or the declaration is strong but lacks a formal deed — community interpretation with documented basis.
low Any unresolved medium-or-higher risk flag, incomplete evidence chain, or refusal-adjacent case. Do not build commercially without independent legal review.

Risk flags are never suppressed — only documented. If the generator cannot serve a prompt safely from the corpus, it produces a refusal object instead of a world bible. Every compliance manifest carries the full flag list regardless of confidence level.

For agents

Worldkit exposes a JSON API at /api/corpus and /api/world/{id}. World Bibles include a machine-readable compliance manifest with evidence IDs, risk flags, and computed confidence — structured for agents that need to reason about IP provenance before generating derivative work.
